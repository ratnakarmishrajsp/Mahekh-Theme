How it's crafted

Clay Baked Over Fire

Every bottle of **First Rain Attar** is born from pure earth. Artisans in Kannauj gather clean river clay, bake it in wood ovens, and place the baked clay inside copper pots ("Degs").

As it boils, steam holding the exact molecules of wet earth travels through bamboo pipes into receivers containing a base of pure Sandalwood oil. This ancient, chemical-free hydro-distillation method is the only way to bottle the real smell of monsoon rain.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this attar smell exactly like mitti ki khushboo?
Yes. It captures the exact, authentic scent of wet earth (Mitti ki Khushboo) meeting the first rain shower. Since we distil genuine clay baked in traditional copper pots in Kannauj, it smells organic, calming, and highly nostalgic.
Is it completely safe for direct skin application?
Absolutely. Our attars are 100% natural, alcohol-free, and chemical-free. Sourced directly from Kannauj using pure sandalwood base, they are safe, soothing, and moisturizing for all skin types, including sensitive skin.
Will the attar stain my clothes?
Our attar is oil-concentrated, so we recommend dabbing it directly on your skin pulse points (neck, wrists). Avoid applying directly on delicate, light-colored fabrics to prevent any temporary oil spots.
